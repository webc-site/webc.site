name: doc_style
---

Write formally and avoid spoken language: Use a rigorous and professional speaking style for official technical documents, and prohibit the use of modal particles or everyday spoken language in the document.

Eliminate third-person pronouns: It is prohibited to use third-person pronouns such as "it" and "they" to refer to tools, scripts or modules. They should directly refer to "execution time", "the script", "this tool" or the specific name.

Be realistic about functions and avoid frills: When describing tools or scripts, avoid using marketing or abstract modifiers such as "intelligent" and "advanced" and directly describe their specific functions and uses.

Control sentence length and use line breaks: avoid writing long, complex sentences. Reasonably split into short sentences, and use lists and multiple line breaks to optimize the reading and visual layout experience.

Reduce foreign terms and localize translation: Use as few English terms as possible and use plain Chinese explanations (for example: use "code difference" instead of "Git diff" or "diff"; use "commit information" instead of "Commit message").
